About

Michigan Carbon Capture is the University of Michigan's student organization dedicated to innovation and education in carbon capture technology.

As the student branch of the Global CO2 Initiative, we coordinate both research initiatives to develop novel carbon capture technology and educational initiatives to advance climate tech understanding in the Ann Arbor community.

Project SAPPHIRE

Project SAPPHIRE is MCC's interdisciplinary student team working to build a functional MECC cell to extract carbon and hydrogen gas from urban wastewater. Based in the U-M Wilson Center, Project SAPPHIRE has spent the last two years reiterating on a novel design that can encourage carbon capture and green energy generation from unlikely places.
